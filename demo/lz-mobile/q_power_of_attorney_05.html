Your agent's powers.

You can grant your agent the power to make various types of decisions on your behalf. Please select which powers you'd like to give to your agent. (Check all that apply.)

The power to buy, sell, rent and manage property, take out mortgages, and execute deeds.

The power to buy, sell and manage all types of goods such as vehicles, furniture, jewelry and other types of personal property.

The power to sign checks, withdraw funds, open accounts, borrow money and remove items from your safe deposit box.

The power to buy, sell, and exchange stocks, bonds and mutual funds, and vote as a shareholder.

The power to manage, operate and sell any businesses you own, including partnerships and closely-held corporations; to buy or expand businesses; and to exercise rights as a business partner or bond holder.

The power to manage your retirement and pension plans, including exercising investment powers, designating beneficiaries, making contributions, and borrowing against or selling assets.

The power to take out insurance policies for yourself or your family, modify them, borrow against them, and surrender them for their present cash value.
