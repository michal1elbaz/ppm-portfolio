Your agent's powers.

You can grant your agent the power to make various types of decisions on your behalf. Please select which powers you'd like to give to your agent. (Check all that apply.)

The power to act in all matters pertaining to a trust, estate or other fund from which I am entitled, and the power to transfer property to your living trust (if you have one).

The power to hire attorneys for you, act for you in legal matters, sign legal documents, and sue on your behalf.

The power to collect social security, Medicare, Medicaid, and any other government benefits on your behalf.

The power to file and sign tax returns, represent you in all tax matters, and receive confidential information and refund checks.

The power to take care of your spouse and children and to maintain their accustomed standard of living, including paying for health care, education, food and housing.

The power to make gifts to others from my property and assets. You will later have the option of restricting your agent from making gifts to himself or herself.

The power to take care of your pets and animals and to maintain their accustomed standard of living, including paying for shelter, food, and veterinary care.
