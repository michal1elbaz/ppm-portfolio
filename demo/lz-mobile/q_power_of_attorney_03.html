Joint Power of Attorney

Please note that we do not prepare joint powers of attorney. A joint power of attorney is where two people, such as a husband and wife, share the same power of attorney. The more common approach is to make two powers of attorney that "mirror" each other. In essence, you both choose the same attorney-in-fact, and choose the same options for this person to carry out on your behalf. Thus, both of your wishes are handled by the same person and in regards to the same matters.
